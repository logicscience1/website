14/06/20 Initial Commit and Website Introduction

This is the first blog post for the website. Not sure what the direction of the blog will take but probably mostly musing on whatever thing am learning or developing at the moment prior to more formal publishing or just to get my thoughts in a row.

This website is meant to be a personal and technical publication of the things am interested in and producing the website itself is a thing am interested in. For example, I manage the content on GitHub and pull it onto my home server running Apache on Linux for publication. I create pages from a template initially in LibreOffice then edit them in Visual Studio Code. The next thing I want to incorporate are HTML pages from R that will probably appear on the dataOps section of the website but could appear in the blog too.

Since a Blog however is meant to be something of a diary of what’s going on; the next thing I’ll be concentrating on is a boot camp for Microsoft DP200 and DP201 over the next 5 days from tomorrow. I did a boot camp for AZ900 before which led to passing the exam but that wasn’t too difficult because am already administrator of an Azure subscription at work and personally responsible for managing a VM (with SQL server express, ODBC to a production Service Now instance and a Power BI gateway), blob storage, file storage and text analytics service. These are all used as part of the data operations day to day. However, we’ve got a lot of different diverse sources of data in the services division that am part of and we really need a big data solution so I’ll be looking at how Azure Data Platform can be leveraged. In the first instance, passing the exams for Microsoft Certified: Azure Data Engineer Associate.

I mention Service Now because that’s the ITSM tool used globally by Insight who I work for and I was involved in the implementation project as the EMEA product owner. That was a great project which involved spending time with the US service operations team in Arizona working out how we will implement Service Now across the business globally. At the time, I was administrator of service Aide for the EMEA service desk, so I planned the data migration and implemented the export from service Aide as well as describing all our ITIL processes to the US team so we could have a unified set of processes globally. We solved some good problems on that export, one in particular was the export of images from the knowledge base and the code for that is somewhere on my GitHub.

I had initially joined Insight as a transition and change manager so I wore a number of hats supporting the organisational development as a service oriented company. A great pleasure of this role was successfully project managing opening up a new office and service desk in Madrid, Spain. I had a background in that sort of thing from a previous job on the management team transitioning and training a service desk in Belgrade, Serbia for NCR. I also had a lot of experience transitioning big customers such as Boots and Morrisons into service as well as service desk management and problem management for the big customers including Boots, Walmart, Morrisons and Sainsbury. Prior to leaving, I was also admin on SAP business analytics platform which was a layer on their Terradata data warehouse because I produced a lot of service monitoring reports for the business. In particular, over a two year period we were supporting Sainsbury migrating their POS solution which involved multiple testing upgrade and deployment phases, throughout this I was providing analysis and predictive analytics for incident volume and service desk impact.

The most interesting part of the Madrid implementation for Insight though was conducting the due diligence for the location; doing analysis of costs, risks and benefits of opening a business in different European countries. Then getting the opportunity to talk to the service desk on their first week about service management, data quality and the significant impact that IT support can have on the development of technology. However, the main outcome of two years of effort with my current employer is finding my place as services data operations specialist after getting a deep knowledge of the company data structures and processes. It’s been my good fortune to also receive leadership training in the organisation so that I’m in a good position to lead the communications for data operations.

I also mention Power BI because that’s what we use at work for analysing and presenting data. I enjoy the challenge of keeping up with Microsoft’s development of this application and it’s associated publishing service. It’s a good way of sharing reports and in many respects makes analysis easy although I still find myself in some cases preferring R or python. Luckily these can also be integrated with Power BI. I think by now I’ve published over 50 reports in Power BI so we’ll be going through a process of identifying the important and reusable metrics for the business from all these reports and consolidating them into a single set of reports more aligned with the dataOps function.

So at this moment in time am looking forward to getting to know the Azure data platform and coming up later in the year I’m starting more learning when I return to my statistics study with the Open University for their Graduate Certificate in Theoretical Statistics and Probability. All this to develop my specialism in dataOps and a chance to play with code for the translation of data into communications, information, prediction and prescriptive analytics. All being well, I’ll be able to keep it together and use this website to present and talk about it. As a concerned parent of the next generation I also like to keep an eye on what technology is doing and in particular things like education, artificial intelligence, data privacy, augmented reality and IoT. So these are also topics am likely to comment on in the blog.
