I’ve basically been a computer enthusiast ever since my old dad used to bring computers home from the school he worked at to experiment with. He was a chemistry teacher and they were always looking for ways to interface with experiments and for analysing results. He first brought home the ZX Spectrum 48k then the UK the schools adopted the BBC micro which I used throughout high school. Using the manual I taught myself BASIC programming and the rudiments of tagged document layout before the days of HTML.

On this device with it’s large floppy disks I built a rudimentary database application n BASIC long before I knew what a database even was. I used it to store my collection of football matchday programs and to compile lists for sale or swap with other collectors who at that time were still using photo copied typed lists.

So computers for me have always been a tool for collecting and distributing information and that’s the work I find myself specialising in these days having been an IT professional since 2008 when my first job was an MS access developer and analyst. Supplementing my technical skills on IT infrastructure and development I’m also a data scientist with skills in statistics making me an all round dataOps specialist with experience in data management, data engineering, data science and data communication.

In recent times I’m following the trend to the cloud and building a skill set in Microsoft Azure infrastructure and data platform.

Employment CV available on request which documents over a decade experience in IT service management, problem management, process management and analysis evolved into data operations specialist for IT services.
